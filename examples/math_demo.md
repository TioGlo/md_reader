# LaTeX Math Demo

## Inline Math

The quadratic formula is $x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$, which gives the solutions to $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$.

Einstein's famous equation $E = mc^2$ relates energy and mass. The Pythagorean theorem states that $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$.

## Display Math

The Gaussian integral:

$$\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-x^2} dx = \sqrt{\pi}$$

Euler's identity:

$$e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0$$

A matrix equation:

$$\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} ax + by \\ cx + dy \end{pmatrix}$$

## Maxwell's Equations

$$\nabla \cdot \mathbf{E} = \frac{\rho}{\epsilon_0}$$

$$\nabla \cdot \mathbf{B} = 0$$

$$\nabla \times \mathbf{E} = -\frac{\partial \mathbf{B}}{\partial t}$$

$$\nabla \times \mathbf{B} = \mu_0 \mathbf{J} + \mu_0 \epsilon_0 \frac{\partial \mathbf{E}}{\partial t}$$

## Summations and Series

The Taylor series for $e^x$:

$$e^x = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{x^n}{n!}$$

The Basel problem:

$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2} = \frac{\pi^2}{6}$$
